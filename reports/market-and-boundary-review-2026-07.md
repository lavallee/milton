# Milton market and boundary review

**Review date:** 2026-07-17
**Decision use:** re-ground `VISION.md`, `ROADMAP.md`, ownership boundaries,
and the `plans/milton-product/` work packets
**Evidence posture:** original product/repository documentation, current local
code, existing Projector research, and explicit inference. No vendor efficacy
claim was independently benchmarked.

## Executive decision

Milton remains worth building, but not under its original novelty claims. The
goal is coherent factory infrastructure that is useful to others, not a thin
integration layer optimized to manufacture a venture-style unique value
proposition.

Local coding-agent trace analysis exists. Cross-session categorization and
failure clustering exist. Cost per commit/PR exists. Memory runtimes record
reads and automatically age retained material. Milton's opportunity is the
combined contract none of this review's verified offerings demonstrates:

> heterogeneous post-hoc coding-agent evidence → conservative cost
> reconciliation → real outcome attribution → graded operational finding →
> authoritative action receipt.

The architectural consequence is equally important: Milton becomes the
retrospective projection and finding owner, not a new canonical warehouse for
Somm, Fab, George, Projector, memory runtimes, or Spindle.

## Questions commissioned

Three independent read-only lanes were commissioned under the Forge planning
and quick-turn research discipline:

1. **Competitive scan:** current semantic synthesis, local coding-log analysis,
   cost-per-outcome, and memory-hygiene offerings.
2. **Complement map:** authoritative boundaries and smallest integration
   contracts across Projector, Flip, George, Fab, Forge, Chip, Spindle, and
   Keel.
3. **Somm audit:** current hot-path/accounting/eval/recommendation behavior,
   duplication/gap risks, and a retain/narrow/delegate/deprecate decision.

The existing Projector reports
[software-factory landscape](../../projector/reports/software-factory-landscape-2026-07.md),
[chip/workflow primitives](../../projector/reports/chip-workflow-primitives-2026-07.md),
and the [Fable red-team](../../projector/reports/chip-spec-fable-red-team-2026-07.md)
were treated as prior evidence rather than repeated.

A fourth independent read-only red-team reviewed the resulting documentation
package. It forced four material corrections before this report closed:
current versus target integration is labeled explicitly; refutation is
adjudication rather than acted-on; historical action receipts survive temporary
coverage loss; and the itemized dependency graph blocks relation-emitting work
on the relation contract.

## Somm deep audit

### Retain in Somm

- mediated call ledger and token-times-price observation;
- daily workload admission, plan pacing, quota inference/application;
- model pricing/intel, call/workload frontier, datasets, eval receipts,
  shadow-eval ownership, campaigns, bounded routing recommendations, and
  model-choice decision memory;
- source-local call/attempt failure classification and eval/error summaries;
- one-attempt harness execution and normalization; and
- source-local `somm spend/status/plans` operational views, explicitly scoped.

### Delegate or narrow

- cross-source reconciliation and cost-per-Fab/George/Git/PR outcome → Milton;
- future historical model+harness task-outcome memory → Milton projection,
  consumed by Somm routing;
- general failure motifs, behavioral drift, memory hygiene, and procedures →
  Milton;
- Milton may supply cross-source outcome evidence, but quota inference,
  live-plan pacing, and policy application remain in Somm;
- historical/all-project views must say “Somm-recorded amount,” expose
  provenance/kind coverage, and never claim whole-system/provider-billed truth.

### P0 producer defects

#### Shadow-eval spend is not a first-class call

Current gold and judge provider requests bypass the `calls` ledger in
`../somm/packages/somm-service/src/somm_service/workers/shadow_eval.py`.
Their cost is embedded or estimated in eval JSON; the gold estimate uses the
production call's token counts rather than the gold response's actual tokens.

Required correction: persist production, `shadow_gold`, and `shadow_judge` as
separate call observations using returned usage; link them to eval receipts;
make campaign/eval cost a rollup over child ids.

#### OTLP round trips can duplicate a billed call

The exporter sends `somm.call_id`, while OTLP ingest derives a new id from
trace/span and ignores that coordinate. Exporting a call back into the same
Somm ledger can therefore insert a second call and cost.

Required correction: existing `somm.call_id` is idempotent; absent upstream
ids are preserved and rows marked foreign/imported; foreign rows do not affect
budgets or recommendations by default; exporter→wire→ingester is tested as one
round trip.

### Other gaps to preserve in plan

- no first-class provider request/billing id;
- normal `cost_kind` and pricing version remain mostly unknown;
- late `SommResult.mark()` outcomes are not persisted into current views;
- global call mirroring can duplicate primaries for naive consumers;
- plan/budget SQL must not treat notional subscription value as marginal cash;
  and
- campaign/recommendation totals are rollups, never billable observations.

The proposed commission is
`plans/milton-product/commissions/somm-accounting-integrity.md`.

## Adversarial critique

- The conjunctive wedge may be too broad to explain. The two MVP proofs must be
  independently useful; “we do everything together” is not positioning.
- Conservative attribution may yield a large unallocated bucket. That is a
  product finding, not permission to add fuzzy joins; the roadmap explicitly
  permits narrowing to runner outcomes.
- Findings can create the same attention debt they diagnose. Precision and
  queue-reduction gates are product requirements.
- The local ecosystem can become integration-heavy. A consumer contract without
  a live end-to-end checkpoint is dead architecture.
- Model-assisted clustering may be obsolete before it ships. The direct versus
  pipeline experiment is a kill gate, not ceremonial evaluation.
- Narrowing Somm's role creates a gap if Milton is unavailable on the request
  path. The dependency direction forbids this: Somm's local controls and facts
  remain independently sufficient.
- Vertical integration reduces dependency and lock-in exposure but increases
  owned maintenance. Revisit a build decision when a mature, completely open,
  replaceable component is materially harder or safer than our implementation;
  do not revisit merely because a startup offers a polished hosted surface.

## Decisions and next gates

1. Adopt the re-grounded `VISION.md` and gated `ROADMAP.md`.
2. Build directed relations and action-receipt projection before more adapters.
3. Run cost-per-outcome and stale-gate vertical proofs.
4. Accept the Somm/Fab/George commissions in their target repos before dispatch.
5. Do not begin generic clustering, broad memory work, or procedure export
   until the MVP checkpoints graduate.
6. Apply `docs/build-vs-adopt.md` before importing any market-map component;
   inspiration does not require dependency, and a public repository alone does
   not satisfy the gate.

This report itself authorizes no external dispatch, merge, publication, gate
retirement, or source-system mutation.
